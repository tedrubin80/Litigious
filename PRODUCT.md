# LegalEstate

## Product Purpose
A white-label legal practice management platform sold as licensable modules to law firms, legal tech resellers, and enterprise legal departments. Firms buy the core platform and add modules (billing, AI documents, case management, medical records, etc.) as needed. Resellers can rebrand and resell under their own name.

## Register
product

## Users

**Primary buyers (license holders):**
- Law firm administrators (2-50 attorney firms) evaluating practice management software
- Legal tech resellers who white-label and resell to their own clients
- Enterprise legal departments at corporations

**Daily users inside the platform:**
- Attorneys (case work, time tracking, research)
- Paralegals (document prep, client intake, task management)
- Legal assistants (scheduling, client communication)
- Clients (portal access: case status, documents, billing)

**User context:** Professionals working 8-12 hour days in high-stakes environments. They need speed and clarity, not delight. They do NOT trust software that looks like it was built last month. Errors cost them cases.

## Modules (white-label units)

1. **Core** (always required) — Auth, roles, dashboard, client management
2. **Case Management** — Full lifecycle, statuses, timelines, case types
3. **Document & AI** — AI-powered document generation, multi-provider (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.)
4. **Billing & Time** — Time tracking, invoicing, payment processing (Stripe, PayPal, Square, BNPL)
5. **Medical Records** — Medical tracking for personal injury / workers' comp cases
6. **Communications** — Internal chat, client messaging, notifications
7. **Video Conferencing** — WebRTC + Zoom dual-platform meetings
8. **Legal Research** — Lex Machina integration, case outcome analytics
9. **Reports & Analytics** — Practice performance, financial reporting, custom dashboards

## Tone

Authoritative, precise, understated. Like a well-run law firm: no unnecessary words, no flash. Competence communicated through clarity, not decoration. The platform should feel like it was built by people who understand law, not by a startup trying to look legal-adjacent.

## Anti-references

- Clio, MyCase, Smokeball — generic blue legal SaaS, indistinguishable sidebar layouts
- Salesforce Legal — over-engineered, corporate bloat aesthetic
- LegalZoom marketing — consumer-friendly but cheapens the brand
- Generic startup SaaS: gradient heroes, hero metrics (big number + small label + gradient accent), glassmorphism cards
- Navy + gold "prestige law" aesthetic — first reflex, immediately recognizable as AI-generated legal branding

## Strategic Principles

1. **Module clarity over feature piling.** Each module should have a clear value proposition that can be explained in one sentence. Avoid feature sprawl within a module.
2. **White-label readiness.** Branding (name, colors, logo) must be swappable at the firm/reseller level. No hardcoded brand references in the core UI shell.
3. **Professional-grade density.** Legal professionals work with a lot of data. Prefer dense, information-rich layouts over spacious consumer UX. Tables over card grids.
4. **Trust signals over marketing language.** Concrete specifics (case count, document types, integration names) over superlatives ("best-in-class", "powerful", "seamless").
5. **Consistency across modules.** Each module should feel like part of one system, not a patchwork of features. Shared design tokens, shared layout shell.
